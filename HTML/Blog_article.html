New technology and the environmental concern are making the footwear industry change fast. Right now, many shoe brands have begun to use sustainable materials such as recycled plastic bottles, organic cotton and natural rubber when manufacturing shoes. These materials mean shoes are better for the planet: they reduce waste and pollution. "Even some companies are deploying creative materials like mushroom leather and pineapple fibres in place of animal leather," said Dr. Wei Yanjun. They are eco friendly and save resources from going waste and help reduce harmful chemicals. Shoes made from sustainable materials will generally last longer and when they are thrown away, they will break down over time protecting the earth. It also helps to make shoes in this way as it uses less water and energy meaning saving natural resources. More brands are creating programmes that inspire customers to recycle used shoes, making the entire operation greener. Modern footwear is therefore becoming more eco-friendly, more comfortable and no less stylish, thanks to overall more sustainable materials. When we select these shoes, we can contribute to saving the environment, being kinder to this planet we live in and help with creating a better future for life on Earth.
